About Shree Radha Krishna Temple

Our History

The Shree Radha Krishna Temple was established in 1957 on 31 Botanic Road in Liverpool and was inaugurated by His Excellency, High Commissioner of India. In 1978, the temple was relocated to its current location at 253 Edge Lane. Over the years, the temple has grown into a vibrant community center, serving the spiritual needs of thousands of devotees. The temple's architecture reflects traditional Hindu design, with a serene atmosphere conducive to prayer and meditation.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide a welcoming and inclusive environment for all devotees to connect with Lord Krishna and Radha and deepen their spiritual practice. We aim to promote the teachings of the Vedas and Upanishads, fostering a sense of community and spiritual growth among our members. The temple offers a variety of services, including daily prayers, special pujas, and educational programs for all ages.

Spiritual Purpose

The Shree Radha Krishna Temple serves as a center for spiritual learning and practice, focusing on the worship of Lord Krishna and Radha, the embodiments of divine love and devotion. Devotees come to the temple to seek blessings, guidance, and inner peace through prayer, meditation, and participation in religious ceremonies. The temple's spiritual purpose is to help individuals realize their inner potential and live a life of purpose and fulfillment through devotion to Lord Krishna.
